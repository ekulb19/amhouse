New Section ‚Äî Sub-Branding Strategy

Premium Sub-Branding

Brand architecture models for Somerset, Lakeshore & Bloomfield premium properties

Why Sub-Branding Matters

Problem 1: Price-Perception Mismatch

Premium properties charging premium rates under a brand perceived as "affordable" creates friction in the sales process.

Problem 2: Marketing Inefficiency

A single brand message cannot simultaneously communicate "affordable family care" and "luxury senior living."

Problem 3: Capital Perception

Investors may undervalue premium assets because the parent brand doesn't signal premium positioning.

Industry Models

Watermark ‚Äî Tiered Collections

Operates √âlan Collection and √âlite Collection ‚Äî distinct brand identities within the portfolio. Each collection has its own brand book, visual standards, positioning statements, and ad copy. Allows targeting different price segments while maintaining operational efficiency.

Source: BMC Luxury Collective case study

Senior Lifestyle ‚Äî Sub-Brand Integration

Three sub-brands with unique website designs under one parent site. Result: +31.6% leads, -15.9% CPL. Key was giving each tier its own visual identity while keeping parent brand visible for trust.

Source: Senior Housing News (March 2022)

Recommended Options for American House

Option A: "Collections" Model

American House (parent) + American House Premier Collection or "[Name] by American House"

Lowest risk Fastest to implement

Maintains heritage equity while signaling elevated experience. Distinct visual identity for premium properties.

Option B: Standalone Premium

American House (standard) + "Somerset Living" or "Lakeshore Living" as independent brands

Strongest premium signal Higher investment

Highest brand separation. Requires separate marketing infrastructure. "From the creators of American House" endorsement.

Option C: Endorsed Architecture

"Somerset, an American House Community" ‚Äî property name leads, parent brand endorses

Most flexible Acquisition-ready

Allows each premium property to develop local identity. Flexible for future acquisitions that may not fit AH brand.

Recommendation

Option A or C provides the best risk-reward for AH's current scale. Option A is faster and cheaper; Option C is more flexible for growth. The critical success factor is visual differentiation ‚Äî the premium tier must look and feel distinctly different online and in-person.

What Boomers Want

"The vast majority of communities operating in the United States were built over 10 years ago and were designed for a different generation ‚Äî not just a different generation of seniors, but a different generation of the resident's adult children and a different generation of team members. Lifestyle, Not Care

Boomers want to continue active lives and start new chapters ‚Äî not "quiet retirement." They seek purpose, connection, and freedom of choice in how they spend their time.

Hospitality, Not Healthcare

The industry is shifting toward hospitality-inspired models. Terms like "care" and "retirement" don't resonate ‚Äî hence the shift from CCRC to "Life Plan Community."

Tech-Enabled & Sustainable

Smart home adoption up 45% in 3 years. Telehealth for senior care projected at $14.5B by 2028. LEED/eco-design increasingly expected.
